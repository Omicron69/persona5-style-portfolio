Mieko Kawakami wrote that "people are strange... they know nothing lasts forever, but still find time to laugh and cry and get upset, laboring over things." My job as an AI Engineer and full-stack developer is to build software that respects that beautifully complex human reality.


I am a Computer Science graduate First-Class Honours currently specializing in machine learning and computer vision through an MSc in Artificial Intelligence at Kingston University London. I combine mathematical precision with human-centered design, translating complex algorithms into intuitive, enjoyable web applications. From building real-time browser gesture recognition models that power a fun, Duolingo-style platform to learn the basics of British Sign Language fingerspelling (bslgame.co.uk), to leading front-end development for stakeholder-focused healthcare data tracking solutions, I specialize in making data pipelines leak-free, scalable, and accessible. You can explore my technical repositories and live deployments at github.com/Omicron69.


There is a genuine excitement that comes from understanding how other people live, eat, and communicate. I thrive on learning about different cultures and food cuisines, and getting to know the textured reality behind them is what keeps me curious about the world. Growing up speaking English, Bengali, Hindi, and Urdu fluently, alongside conversational Japanese, has shaped how I listen and connect with others. That same grounding carried me through the heat of fast-paced hospitality kitchens and the overwhelming energy of coordinating events for three thousand attendees. Those moments taught me how to hold my composure, observe quietly, and bring real empathy and analytical rigor to collaborative engineering teams. Whether I am laboring over the math to train neural networks or studying audience retention metrics to grow my personal YouTube channel to two million views, I am always searching for the person on the other side of the screen. I am driven by the belief that the best technology is built not just for abstract users, but for living, breathing human beings.
